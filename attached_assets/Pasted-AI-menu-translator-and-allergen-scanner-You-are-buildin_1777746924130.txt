AI menu translator and allergen scanner


You are building a full production-grade AI-powered menu management, translation, and allergen information platform for Nairobi upscale restaurants. Read the full brief, expand the vision, present ~20 substantial phases, wait for "next."
The pain:
High-end restaurants in Gigiri, Westlands, Karen, and Muthaiga serve a heavily international clientele — UN staff, expats, diplomats, tourists — yet almost universally have English-only menus with no allergen information, no dietary labels, and no digital format. A French diplomat who doesn't read English well, a guest with a nut allergy, a tourist trying to understand what nyama choma actually is — all have the same experience: confusion and guesswork. EU regulations around allergen disclosure are now a reference point for international visitors who expect this information. Printing new menus every time something changes costs money and takes days. This is a problem every serious restaurant in Nairobi has and none has solved.
What you are building:
A platform where a restaurant uploads their menu (photo, PDF, or typed) and the system uses AI to: translate it into up to 8 languages, identify and flag allergens per dish, suggest dietary labels (vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, halal), and generate a beautiful digital menu accessible via QR code. The restaurant gets a dashboard where they update dishes, prices, and availability in real time — the QR code always serves the current version. When a dish is 86'd tonight, the manager removes it in 30 seconds. No reprinting. No crossed-out items.
Critical nuances:
Allergen detection from a dish description using AI is imperfect — the platform must make clear that AI-identified allergens should be verified by the kitchen, and provide a workflow for the restaurant to confirm or override each allergen flag. Translation quality for menu items must preserve culinary feel — "pan-seared" should not become a clunky literal translation. Swahili menu items (nyama choma, ugali, sukuma wiki) need contextual explanations, not just translations — "Grilled beef, slow-cooked over charcoal" is more useful than a literal Swahili-to-French translation. The QR code menu must look premium — a badly designed digital menu is worse for the restaurant's brand than a paper one. Setup should take under 30 minutes from upload to live QR code.
Expand this. Think about: a dish popularity tracker (which QR menu items are tapped most); an upsell suggestion layer ("customers who view this dish also order..."); a wine pairing recommendation engine; a "tonight's specials" push that updates the digital menu in real time and can be linked in Instagram bio; integration with reservation systems so the pre-arrival email includes a link to the menu; and a hotel version — a hotel with 3 restaurants and a rooftop bar manages all menus from one dashboard.
Hosting: Next.js on Vercel with edge functions for fast QR menu delivery globally (expats may share links with people outside Kenya). Claude API or OpenAI for translation and allergen detection. Menu assets on Cloudinary. Lightweight Railway backend. PostgreSQL.
